Learning Licence

Overview of Learners Licence in India To be able to legally drive a vehicle on Indian roads, you must hold a valid Driving Licence. The first step to obtaining a Driving Licence in India is getting a Learner's Licence. A Learner's Licence is a document issued by the respective state Regional Transport Office (RTO) and Regional Transport Authority (RTO) and acts as a provisional & restricted Licence. One must be familiar with road sense and rules to get a Learner's licence.

In India, Learner's licence is issued to the candidate after he passes a written or online test that will test his knowledge of road rules. After you get a Learner's licence, you will be allowed to practise driving on public roads provided that you are accompanied by a person who holds a permanent Driving Licence. You must obtain a Learner's licence for the pertinent class of vehicle before you can get a Driving Licence.

Applicants using Aadhaar authentication can take the LL Test from their home or any preferred location, no need to visit RTO. Password for Online LL Test will be sent as SMS to the registered mobile number linked with Aadhaar. Successful applicant can download his/her Learner License. Applicant without Aadhaar Authentication OR Applicant of Non Karnataka Aadhaar holder need to visit RTO Office for document verification and LL Test in person.
